| Layer                        | Public-domain Catholic source                                                            | What it contributes                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Direct commentary            | **Haydock Catholic Bible Commentary**, 1859                                              | Verse-level Catholic interpretation assembled from Haydock, Challoner, Calmet, Menochius, Tirinus, Worthington and others                                                                                         |
| Scriptural whole             | **Douay–Rheims/Challoner** and Clementine Vulgate                                        | Shows that Psalm 21 moves from abandonment and mockery to praise, conversion and worldwide proclamation                                                                                                           |
| Patristic Psalms             | **St Augustine, Expositions on the Psalms**, NPNF translation                            | Christ speaks both personally and through His Mystical Body: the Head and suffering members                                                                                                                       |
| Patristic Gospels            | **Aquinas, Catena Aurea**, Newman’s 1841–45 translation                                  | Verse-by-verse chain of Fathers surrounding Matthew 27 and Mark 15; Newman explains the catena as a chain of patristic passages. [Catena history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catena_%28biblical_commentary%29) |
| Traditional Psalm commentary | **St Robert Bellarmine, Commentary on the Psalms**, John O’Sullivan translation, 1866    | Systematic Catholic exegesis with doctrinal and moral interpretation                                                                                                                                              |
| Gospel commentary            | **Cornelius à Lapide, Great Commentary**, Mossman translations, nineteenth century       | Dense typological, patristic, theological and symbolic connections                                                                                                                                                |
| Liturgical interpretation    | **Dom Guéranger, The Liturgical Year**                                                   | Connects Scripture to Mass, Office, season, feast, ritual and devotional experience; particularly rich for Passiontide and Holy Week. [Work overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Liturgical_Year)          |
| Divine Office                | **Roman Breviary**, Marquis of Bute translation, 1879                                    | Antiphons, psalms, lessons, Fathers and homilies as the Church actually juxtaposed them liturgically                                                                                                              |
| Doctrine                     | **Roman Catechism**, Donovan translation, 1829                                           | Doctrinal guardrails around Passion, redemption, prayer, providence and Christ’s two natures                                                                                                                      |
| Scholastic synthesis         | **Aquinas, Summa Theologiæ**, English Dominican translation, 1911–20                     | Distinctions such as suffering versus despair, God’s presence versus felt consolation, and Christ’s human knowledge and will                                                                                      |
| Ascetical application        | **Spiritual Exercises of St Ignatius**, Mullan translation, 1914                         | Imaginative contemplation, colloquy before the Cross, and movement from observation into personal response                                                                                                        |
| Mystical complement          | **St John of the Cross**, Lewis/Zimmerman translations                                   | Felt divine absence, purification and the difference between experiential darkness and actual abandonment                                                                                                         |
| Personal spirituality        | **St Francis de Sales**, *Introduction to the Devout Life* and *Treatise on Divine Love* | Converts high theology into ordinary affective and practical response                                                                                                                                             |
| Christ-centred spirituality  | **The Imitation of Christ**, older Challoner editions                                    | Conformity to Christ, endurance, consolation and apparent abandonment                                                                                                                                             |
| Catholic ontology            | **Original Catholic Encyclopedia**, 1907–14                                              | Topic definitions, historical figures, doctrines, devotions and cross-domain vocabulary; the original edition is public domain. [Catholic Encyclopedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Encyclopedia)      |
